Day 1 – April 3: Territories, Knowledge and Community Leadership

The first day focuses on grounding the gathering in territorial realities, highlighting Indigenous leadership, ancestral knowledge, and community governance systems. Participants will explore how territories are defended, managed, and sustained across different regions of the world.

  • Opening Ceremony: Welcome remarks, cultural rituals, and introduction to the gathering.
  • Keynote Session: Global perspectives on territorial governance and Indigenous leadership.
  • Thematic Panel: Experiences from communities defending land, water, and biodiversity.
  • Dialogue Circles: Small-group exchanges to share regional challenges and solutions.
  • Interactive Workshops: Tools and methodologies for strengthening community governance.
  • Community Showcase: Presentations of grassroots initiatives and territorial projects.
  • Cultural Gathering: Music, storytelling, and traditional expressions from participating communities.

Day 2 – April 4: Collaboration, Innovation and Territorial Strategies

The second day explores collaborative approaches, innovation, and practical strategies for addressing climate change, biodiversity loss, and territorial threats. It emphasizes co-creation, partnerships, and learning across regions.

  • Keynote Session: Climate justice and nature-based solutions led by communities.
  • Thematic Panels: Community conservation, biodiversity protection, and sustainable livelihoods.
  • Workshops: Participatory mapping, territorial monitoring, and data governance tools.
  • Innovation Labs: Spaces for co-designing solutions to shared territorial challenges.
  • Networking Sessions: Facilitated exchanges between organizations, leaders, and allies.
  • Initiatives Fair: Exhibition of projects, tools, and community-led innovations.
  • Evening Reflection: Collective dialogue on lessons learned and emerging ideas.

Day 3 – April 5: Global Alliances and Future Action

The final day is dedicated to building long-term alliances, strengthening networks, and defining collective actions. Participants will work together to shape shared commitments and future collaboration pathways.

  • Keynote Session: Building global alliances for territorial protection.
  • Panel Discussion: Women and youth leadership in territorial defense.
  • Dialogue Circles: Identifying priorities for regional and global collaboration.
  • Collaborative Session: Co-creation of a shared declaration and action roadmap.
  • Closing Plenary: Presentation of key outcomes and commitments.
  • Closing Ceremony: Cultural closing, acknowledgments, and next steps.

  • Shared declaration for territorial protection
  • Stronger global networks
  • Collaborative initiatives
  • Increased visibility for community conservation
